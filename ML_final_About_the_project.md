# **‚úÖ What is EEG?**

**EEG (Electroencephalography)** is a non-invasive technique used to record the brain‚Äôs electrical activity using small sensors (electrodes) placed on the scalp.  
 It measures tiny voltage changes generated by large groups of brain cells (neurons). These signals help doctors and researchers study brain function, diagnose neurological disorders, and analyze patterns such as brain-wave frequencies (alpha, beta, theta, delta).

EEG is especially useful for conditions like epilepsy, Alzheimer‚Äôs disease, sleep disorders, and brain-function research because it shows how the brain behaves in real time.

## **3\. Meaning of the components (so you can interpret)**

To work effectively with this dataset (especially for building machine learning or signal‚Äêprocessing pipelines) you should understand what each of the components *mean*, what the key terms are. Here are the major terms/components and their meanings:

### **3.1 EEG (Electroencephalography)**

* EEG is a method of recording electrical activity of the brain through electrodes placed on the scalp. It reflects the collective synaptic activity (mainly postsynaptic potentials) of large populations of neurons.

* In this dataset it is resting‚Äêstate, eyes closed: subjects are lying (or sitting) quietly with eyes closed and no task being performed; this gives a baseline ‚Äúbrain rhythm‚Äù measure.

* The 19 electrodes follow the ‚Äú10‚Äì20 international system‚Äù for scalp EEG. So Fp1, Fp2, F7 etc denote specific scalp locations.

* Sampling rate 500 Hz means the EEG signal is sampled (digitised) 500 times per second; so frequency analyses up to e.g. \~250 Hz (nyquist) are possible (though brain rhythms of interest often \< 50 Hz).

* Preprocessing steps (filtering, artifact removal, ICA) are needed because EEG is noisy (eye blinks, muscle activity, line noise). The cleaned/preprocessed data support more reliable analyses.

### **3.2 BIDS (Brain Imaging Data Structure)**

* BIDS is a standard for organising neuroimaging data (MRI, EEG, MEG) so that file/folder names and metadata are consistent. Using BIDS means easier interoperability with analysis tools.

* In this dataset, raw and/or preprocessed EEG data are BIDS‚Äêcompliant. So you‚Äôll find standard file names like `sub-01_ses-01_task-rest_eeg.set` (or similar) and sidecar `‚Ä¶_eeg.json` with metadata.

* Participants metadata in `participants.tsv`, giving e.g. age, sex, group.

### **3.3 Diagnostic groups: Alzheimer‚Äôs disease & Frontotemporal dementia**

* **Alzheimer‚Äôs disease (AD):** The most common form of dementia, a progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterised by memory loss (especially short‚Äêterm memory), eventual impairment of language/orientation/self‚Äêcare. Neuropathologically associated with amyloid-Œ≤ plaques, neurofibrillary tangles (tau), brain atrophy (especially hippocampus and cortex), and changes in network connectivity. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Frontotemporal dementia (FTD):** A group of neurodegenerative disorders primarily affecting frontal and/or temporal lobes of the brain, often with behavioural/personality changes or language impairment (depending on subtype) rather than primarily memory loss. FTD is less common than AD. [MDPI+1](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Healthy Control (HC):** Subjects without neurological diagnosis of dementia; serve as baseline/reference group.

### **3.4 Key EEG‚Äêanalysis terms that appear in research using this dataset**

Since you may build ML/AI pipelines on this data, knowing these terms helps:

* **Power spectrum / spectral analysis:** EEG signals can be decomposed into frequency components (e.g., delta \<4 Hz, theta \~4-8 Hz, alpha \~8-12/13 Hz, beta \~13-30 Hz, gamma \>30 Hz). Many studies on dementia look for changes in these bands. [PubMed+1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Alpha/theta‚Äêratio:** Ratio of power in the alpha band vs power in theta band; in this dataset, controls had significantly higher alpha/theta ratio than AD or FTD. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Aperiodic / periodic components of spectrum:** Some recent analyses decompose EEG spectra into (i) ‚Äúaperiodic‚Äù (1/f like) background activity, and (ii) ‚Äúperiodic‚Äù oscillatory peaks. One study referenced this dataset and used such decomposition. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Permutation entropy (PeEn), spectral entropy (SpEn):** Measures of complexity or randomness of EEG signals; used to assess brain function in disease vs healthy. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Spectral edge frequency (SEF):** The frequency below which a certain percentage of total power is contained (for example 95 %); used as a summary measure of EEG spectral distribution. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Montage (electrode arrangement):** The set and arrangement of electrodes; in this dataset 19 scalp electrodes plus two reference electrodes are used. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **3.5 Medical/clinical evaluation terms**

* **Mini‚ÄêMental State Examination (MMSE):** A widely used cognitive screening tool; scores range from 0 to 30, with lower scores indicating more severe cognitive impairment. The dataset includes MMSE scores for participants. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Disease duration (months):** How long the diagnosis has been present or symptoms recognized; for AD group median \~25 months. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Diagnosis criteria:** For AD and FTD, standardized clinical criteria were used (DSM III‚ÄìR, DSM IV, ICD-10, NINCDS-ADRDA for AD) to ensure correct classification. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

## **5\. Medical/Neuroscientific Context ‚Äî what you *need to know***

Since you asked for ‚Äúdisease ... each medical understanding and terms I have to have to understand the process and datasets‚Äù, here is a breakdown of the relevant medical/neuroscience background.

### **5.1 Dementia, Alzheimer‚Äôs disease (AD)**

* **Dementia**: A clinical syndrome characterised by a decline in cognitive function associated with damage or disease in the brain beyond what might be expected from normal ageing. Memory, thinking, orientation, comprehension, calculation, learning capacity, language, and judgement may be impaired.

* Alzheimer‚Äôs disease: The most common cause of dementia (\~60-80 % of cases) in older adults. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com) It is progressive and neurodegenerative.

* Neuropathology: Key features include extracellular amyloid-Œ≤ plaques, intracellular neurofibrillary tangles (tau protein), synaptic dysfunction, neuron loss, and brain atrophy (especially hippocampus, cortex).

* Clinical features: Initially mild memory loss, particularly short‚Äêterm memory, then decline in other cognitive functions (language, reasoning, orientation). Behavioural changes, impaired self‚Äêcare, eventually severe disability. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* EEG in AD: Historically, EEG in AD often shows ‚Äúslowing‚Äù ‚Äî i.e., increased power in slower bands (theta, delta), decreased alpha/beta, reduced alpha/theta ratio, reduced peak alpha frequency. The dataset‚Äôs associated research found such differences. [PubMed+1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **5.2 Frontotemporal dementia (FTD)**

* FTD is a group of disorders caused by progressive nerve cell loss in the frontal lobes and/or temporal lobes of the brain (hence ‚Äúfronto-temporal‚Äù).

* It often presents earlier in age (compared to typical AD), and the initial symptoms may involve changes in behaviour/personality (behavioural variant FTD) or language (primary progressive aphasia variants) rather than memory loss. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* EEG in FTD: FTD may show different EEG patterns (sometimes less pronounced slowing than AD, but other network/connectivity changes). In the dataset‚Äôs associated analysis, FTD had specific EEG feature differences compared to controls and AD. [PubMed+1](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **5.3 Cognitive screening ‚Äì MMSE**

* The Mini‚ÄêMental State Examination (MMSE) is a widely used test of cognitive function. It assesses orientation to time/space, immediate recall, short‚Äêterm memory, language, and attention. Scores range 0‚Äì30; normal often \~27-30; lower scores indicate cognitive impairment.

* In this dataset, controls had around 30, AD \~17.8, FTD \~22.2 on average. [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **5.4 EEG rhythms & brain‚Äêwave frequency bands**

* **Delta** (\<4 Hz) ‚Äì often dominant during deep sleep; increased delta in waking state can indicate pathology.

* **Theta** (\~4-8 Hz) ‚Äì often seen in drowsiness, early sleep; increased theta in waking state may indicate dysfunction or cognitive decline.

* **Alpha** (\~8-12 Hz, sometimes up to \~13 Hz) ‚Äì prominent in awake, relaxed, eyes‚Äêclosed state especially in posterior regions; reductions in alpha power or peak alpha frequency are often markers of brain ageing/dysfunction.

* **Beta** (\~13-30 Hz) ‚Äì associated with active thinking, attention; changes can reflect cortical dysfunction.

* **Gamma** (\>30 Hz) ‚Äì high‚Äêfrequency oscillations, harder to measure cleanly, sometimes linked to cognition/attention.

* In AD/FTD research, one often sees **slowing**: reduced alpha, increased theta/delta, shift of spectral distribution to lower frequencies, lowered alpha/theta ratio. The dataset‚Äôs associated study reported controls had alpha/theta ratio \~2.62 while AD \~0.55 etc. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **5.5 Other EEG‚Äêderived measures you should understand**

* **Entropy (Permutation Entropy, Spectral Entropy):** These measure the complexity or unpredictability of the EEG signal. Lower complexity may correspond to less healthy brain dynamics. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Spectral Edge Frequency (SEF):** The frequency below which a specified percentile (often 95 %) of the total power is located. In slowing conditions, SEF tends to be lower. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* **Aperiodic (1/f) / periodic oscillations decomposition:** Recent EEG work decomposes EEG spectra into a ‚Äúbackground‚Äù aperiodic (1/f) component and distinct oscillatory peaks (periodic). Differences in the aperiodic slope or offset may reflect global network changes in disease. [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40259140/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### **5.6 Why EEG might help in AD/FTD**

* EEG is **non‚Äêinvasive, relatively low cost**, can be repeated frequently, and captures brain functional dynamics (temporal resolution high).

* In neurodegeneration, changes in network connectivity and synaptic function can manifest as altered EEG rhythms or complexity. Thus EEG may provide *biomarkers* for early detection, monitoring progression, or classification of dementia types. The dataset aims to facilitate this. [MDPI+1](https://www.mdpi.com/2306-5729/8/6/95?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

* For machine‚Äêlearning/AI work: The dataset provides labelled data in a standard format, so one can try to extract features and build models that distinguish AD vs HC vs FTD.

# **4\) File extensions and what they store**

* **`.edf`** ‚Äî EEG waveform file (multi-channel time series). Read with MNE or pyEDFlib.

* **`.vhdr`, .vmrk, .eeg\`** ‚Äî BrainVision raw EEG files (header, marker, signal).

* **`.fif`** ‚Äî MNE/Neuromag format (signals \+ metadata).

# **‚úÖ What is Power Spectral Density (PSD)?**

**Power Spectral Density** (PSD) describes **how the power (energy)** of a signal is distributed **across different frequencies**.

For EEG, PSD tells you:

* How much **delta** (0.5‚Äì4 Hz) power the brain is producing

* How much **theta** (4‚Äì8 Hz) power

* How much **alpha** (8‚Äì13 Hz)

* How much **beta** (13‚Äì30 Hz)

* etc.

Think of PSD as converting a **time-domain EEG signal** (voltages over time) into a **frequency-domain representation** (energy over frequency).

---

# **üåü Why PSD is VERY important in dementia EEG**

Research shows:

### **üî∑ Alzheimer‚Äôs Disease:**

* **Delta ‚Üë**

* **Theta ‚Üë**

* **Alpha ‚Üì**

* **Beta ‚Üì**

* **Slowing ratio ‚Üë** (theta+delta)/(alpha+beta)

### **üî∑ Frontotemporal Dementia:**

* Less global slowing than AD

* More changes in **frontal regions**

* Different PSD patterns compared to AD

### **üî∑ Healthy Controls:**

* Normal alpha peak around \~10 Hz

* Balanced theta/alpha/beta distribution

üëâ PSD *directly captures* these differences.

This is why almost **all EEG dementia research uses PSD as core features**.

---

# **üß† Why PSD makes sense mathematically**

EEG raw signal `x(t)` is noisy, non-stationary, and full of artifacts.

PSD converts it into a **stable frequency profile**, revealing:

* Dominant oscillations

* Disease-related spectral slowing

* Network changes

This is crucial because dementia affects **oscillatory rhythms**, not just raw voltage patterns.

---

# **üìå How PSD is computed?**

Common methods:

* **Welch‚Äôs method** (most popular): splitting into small windows, averaging Fourier transforms

* **Multitaper** method

Libraries:

`from mne.time_frequency import psd_welch`  
`psds, freqs = psd_welch(raw)`

You get:

* `freqs` \= frequency axis

* `psds` \= power at each frequency for each channel

You can then compute:

* Band power: integrate PSD over frequency band

* Relative power: band\_power / total\_power

* Ratios: theta/alpha, delta/alpha, etc.
